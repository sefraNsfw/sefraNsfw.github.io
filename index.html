SEFRA

    Search & Discovery Framework for finding not-safe-for-work textual content for Western Balkans languages

    Initial work

    SEFRA – web based framework for searching Web content written in Serbian. SEFRA is an easily customizable hybrid solution that can be a platform for new search applications and/or a service for already existing ones. The proposed architecture solves the problems of indexing, searching and displaying search results adjusted for Serbian. It unifies several web technologies and services into one product suitable for use in the Western Balkan’s countries for helping e-Government citizens services and other public-sector services, private company administration, solving specific search problems for academic institutions and scientific literature publishers, etc. The proposed solution uses advanced Serbian language services accessible over the Web. It is also implementable for any other language where the target language morphology service exists. In other words, architecture is also customizable in this direction. It should be noted that the proposed architecture is optimized from both backend and web front-end perspective. The one application of the proposed architecture is experimentally demonstrated through the search of crime law documents of Serbia. The experimental usage of this implementation shows that the problem of search relevance is well-solved and easily customizable.

    Future work

    Search & Discovery Framework for finding not-safe-for-work textual content for Western Balkans languages (NSFW textual S&D framework) The idea of the project is to provide software framework to be used for the development of Search & Discovery services for not-safe-for-work (NSFW) textual content in the Western Balkans language area. In contrast to the English language and other languages in Western Europe, in Eastern Europe, especially in the Western Balkans, there are no advanced services for search & discovery of textual content (especially not specialized for not-safe-for-work content). Our previous research work has brought framework SEFRA for Serbian language search applications. This framework is open to community and can be adaptable for any specialized textual content types. Therefore, the idea of the project is to advance this framework in the direction of finding NSFW textual content. We are also proposing framework extension in the direction of supporting search & discovery of content written in other Western Balkans languages. After that, we are going to adapt the revised framework for finding NSFW textual content in these Western Balkans languages. The proposed framework will be easily accessed and usable for any type of public or private digital service in Western Balkans area.
